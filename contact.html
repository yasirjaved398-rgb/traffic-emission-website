FAQ

Common questions about traffic emissions

Thousands of vehicles release harmful pollutants every day, reducing air quality and affecting people, animals, and the environment.

Children, older adults, pregnant women, traffic police, drivers, cyclists, street vendors, and people with respiratory or heart conditions are especially vulnerable.

People can use public transport, walk or cycle for short trips, share rides, maintain vehicles, turn off engines while waiting, choose cleaner fuels where available, protect trees, and support pollution-reduction policies.

Effective action includes enforcing emission standards, improving public transport, expanding green spaces, encouraging electric and hybrid vehicles, monitoring air quality, promoting awareness, and improving traffic management.
